The Death Spiral Mechanism: Peloton's subscription platform carries substantial fixed costs (instructor salaries, content production, platform infrastructure, R&D, G&A) that do not decrease proportionally when subscribers churn. As subscribers leave, these fixed costs are spread over a smaller base — raising the cost per subscriber. To maintain margins, Peloton faces pressure to raise prices or cut content quality, which accelerates churn further. This is a demand-driven death spiral. Data Sources & Methodology: All baseline figures sourced from Peloton Interactive, Inc. FY2025 Form 10-K (fiscal year ended June 30, 2025; filed August 7, 2025; SEC Accession No. 0001639825-25-000138).

Key FY2025 figures used: Connected Fitness paid subscriptions: 2,880,000 · App paid subscriptions: ~700,000 · Total subscription cost of revenue: $516.6M · Subscription revenue: ~$1,430M (annualized) · CF avg. monthly price: $44.00 · App avg. monthly price: $12.99 · Monthly churn rate (FY2025): 1.2%.

Fixed vs. Variable Cost Classification: Subscription cost of revenue ($516.6M) is treated as predominantly fixed (instructor salaries, content production, platform infrastructure, music royalties at minimum guarantee levels). A variable component of ~$8/sub/year (payment processing, music overages, customer support) is modeled separately. Connected Fitness fixed cost allocation: ~73% of subscription fixed base (~$377M). App fixed cost allocation: ~27% (~$140M), reflecting lower content intensity. R&D ($174M) and G&A ($284M) are excluded per instructions (not subscription-segment-specific overhead).

Collapse Threshold: Defined as the point at which subscription gross margin turns negative (gross profit < $0). This widget is intended for educational and advisory purposes only. Projections are illustrative.
